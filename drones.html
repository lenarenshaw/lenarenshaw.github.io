Drone Course

In fall, 2017, I TAed CS1951R: Introduction to Robotics. The class consisted of builing, programming, and flying your very own drone from scratch. It was the first time the class had run, and as one of four TAs, developing the class took a lot of effort and time.

My work consisted of developing the final drone students would build, creating assignments, testing projects, holding office hours, and grading. Students completed projects to program the drone using our code. They were written in Python and included position hold and velocity control. Assignments were more theoretical and included networking, debugging, transforms, and motion planning. Check out the website for more details.

You can follow the instructions we provided students with to build your own drone as well. Check out the H2R lab at Brown, and see our feature in the New Yorker. Going forward, we plan on refining the course and putting it online to empower every person with their own robot.
